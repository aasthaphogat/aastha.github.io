Educated - Memoir review

Aastha published on April 10, 2024
2 min, 216 words

Categories: book-review

Tags: book-review blog

The memoir Educated elaborates thorough character development of the author Tara Westover. From being born in an orthodox family in Idaho to embarking on the journey of transformation, cultivating flexibility and openness to new ideas and healing from trauma and complxes.this book is a testament of self exploration.

About

For the first 18 years, the author didn't have any formal education or any birth certificate, her family condemned medical establishments and government as they believed them to be agents of illuminati. It required Tara considerable efforts convincing herself to connect to the real world. This also implies two things: that there's not any one-size-all-fit definition of growth, that everyone have different hardships in life; so does experiences of growth. Tara's elder brother was toxic who inflicted mental and physical tormentation. In the guise of pseudo love for years she believed it to be normal but as she grew, she realised it and untethered herself from insalubrious environment; which speaks the importance of having proclivity of skeptically analysing what is told to you.

Conclusion

The memoir is a thorough insight on character development and the intricacies of education- which includes learning, unlearning, self evaluation and exploration, and becoming a better version of yourself. Tara's journey is bound to leave you feeling inspired. (edited)
